  1. THOR

    • Released in 2011
    • Main Actor

      Chris Hemsworth

    • As the son of Odin (Anthony Hopkins), king of the Norse gods, Thor (Chris Hemsworth) will soon inherit the throne of Asgard from his aging father. However, on the day that he is to be crowned, Thor reacts with brutality when the gods' enemies, the Frost Giants, enter the palace in violation of their treaty. As punishment, Odin banishes Thor to Earth. While Loki (Tom Hiddleston), Thor's brother, plots mischief in Asgard, Thor, now stripped of his powers, faces his greatest threat.

  2. TRANSFORMERS : Age of Extinction

    • Released in 2014
    • Lead Actor

      Mark Wahlberg

    • The fourth live-action Transformers movie, Transformers: Age of Extinction, was released in the United States on June 27, 2014. It stars Mark Wahlberg as Cade Yeager, and also features the voices of Ken Watanabe and John Goodman. The film is about a single father and inventor who discovers a damaged truck that turns out to be a Transformer in disguise. After a great battle, a city lies in ruins but Earth is saved, and a shadowy group emerges to try to take control of history.

  3. AVENGERS : Assemble

    • Released in 2012
    • Lead Actors

      Chris Evans, Chris Hemsworth, and Robert Downey Jr.

    • Marvel's The Avengers, also known as Marvel Avengers Assemble in the United Kingdom and Ireland, was released in theaters on May 4, 2012. It was the final film in Phase One of the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). The film stars Chris Evans, Chris Hemsworth, and Robert Downey Jr., and was directed by Joss Whedon. It has a runtime of 143 minutes and is rated PG-13 for intense sci-fi violence and action, and a mild drug reference.

  4. THE SHAWSHANK REDEMPTION

    • Released in 1994
    • Lead actors

      Tim Robbins and Morgan Freeman

    • The Shawshank Redemption is a 1994 American crime drama film based on Stephen King's novel Rita Hayworth and Shawshank Redemption. The movie stars Tim Robbins and Morgan Freeman and was directed by Frank Darabont. It was released in theaters on January 1, 1994 and had a limited North American release on September 23, 1994. The movie was nominated for seven Academy Awards in 1994, including Best Picture.
